INDIAN FESTIVALS




  1. Diwali


    2. Diwali, also known as the Festival of Lights, is one of the most important Hindu festivals celebrated in India and by Indian communities worldwide. It usually spans five days and symbolizes the victory of light over darkness and good over evil.



  2. Christmas


    3. Christmas is a festive holiday celebrated on December 25th, commemorating the birth of Jesus Christ. It's marked by various traditions, including decorating Christmas trees, exchanging gifts, and gathering with family and friends.



  3. Onam


    4. Onam is a vibrant and culturally rich festival celebrated predominantly in the state of Kerala, India. It usually falls in August or September and marks the harvest season. The festival is linked to the legend of King Mahabali, a beloved ruler who is said to visit his people during Onam.
